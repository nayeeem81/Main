How do we use github project with incremental scrum methodology?

Using GitHub Projects for incremental Scrum means mapping Scrum events 
directly to GitHub’s Kanban views, issue tracking, and iteration 
features. 

It allows your team to plan sprints, track incremental software 
increments (working code shipped after each sprint), and review 
progress within the same platform that hosts your code.

Set Up Your Scrum Workspace:

> To start, create a central board for your development team.

> Create an organization-level or repository-level Project Board to 
serve as your Agile workspace.

> Configure custom fields for your workflow: 
  1. add a Number field for Story Points or Estimates 
  2. and a Select field for Issue Type (e.g., User Story, Task, Bug).

> Manage the Product Backlog (Refinement). The Product Backlog holds 
all your user stories, ordered by business value.

>  Create GitHub Issues for every feature, enhancement, or bug. 

> Group them in a "Table" view and assign Milestones or create Labels 
(e.g., Epic, Priority: High) to categorize work items. 

> Ensure the Product Owner refines these issues by breaking down large 
user stories into smaller, manageable tasks.


1. Sprint Planning:
Sprint planning dictates what your team will work on during the 
upcoming sprint (usually 1–4 weeks). 

2. In your Project settings, configure Iterations (Sprints) with explicit 
start and end dates.

3. Filter your board by the @current or @next iteration. Drag issues 
from the top of the Product Backlog into the Sprint Backlog (Sprint 
Backlog). until the team's capacity (total story points) is reached.

4. Daily Scrum & Development: 
> Your team can use the sprint board to track daily increments of work. 

> Developers move cards across custom status columns like Todo, In 
Progress, In Review, and Done.

> Developers should link their code to specific GitHub issues by 
typing Closes #123 in their commit messages or Pull Requests (PRs). 

> This automates issue tracking and closes the task once merged. 
Conduct a Daily Scrum to discuss blockers and adjust day-to-day 
priorities.

5. Sprint Review & Retrospective: 

> At the end of the sprint, the team demonstrates the incremental product and evaluates processes. Use a 
"Done" view to filter closed issues and evaluate the completed product 
increment during your Sprint Review. 

> Hold a Sprint Retrospective to discuss what went well and what can be 
improved. 

> Update your Project Board workflows and templates based on team 
feedback.
