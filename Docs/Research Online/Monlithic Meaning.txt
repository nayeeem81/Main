Monolithic describes something that is massive, solid, and uniform, often made from a single piece or lacking individual parts. It implies that a person, system, or organization is rigid, vast, and highly resistant to change or new ideas.Common UsagesThe term shifts in meaning depending on the context:Literal / Physical: Refers to objects (like a massive stone or a cast concrete wall) formed from one single, solid piece without joints or seams.Societal or Organizational: Often carries a negative connotation, describing a government, society, or institution that is vast, inflexible, and lacks diversity of thought.Software and Technology: Describes a unified application where all components (user interface, database, business logic) are tightly coupled together in a single codebase.Explore related terminology definitions on Merriam-Webster or browse a wider range of contextual examples on Cambridge Dictionary.
